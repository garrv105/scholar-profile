Quantum Bayesian Inference with Support Vector States for Intrusion Detection

Nayema Mridha, Garrv Sipani, Eva R Gaarder, Shah Haque, Radhika Kuttala, Binay P Akhouri, Mohamad M Al Zein, Eric Howard

A quantum Bayesian inference method for intrusion detection using explicitly constructed quantum circuits and statevector simulation. Prior and conditional probabilities are encoded via unitary gates, and posterior distributions are extracted through symbolic post-selection. Applied to scenarios with network spikes, system vulnerabilities, and false alarms, the method yields joint, marginal, and conditional probabilities aligned with causal structure — demonstrating feasibility and interpretability of quantum-native inference for information security.
